### Alert Details

The National Weather Service (NWS) in Anchorage, AK, has issued a **Heavy Freezing Spray Warning** for the Saint Matthew Island Waters. This alert is classified as a severe threat with a high level of certainty. Additionally, a Storm Warning is in effect for Tuesday.

### Affected Areas

The warning specifically impacts the **Saint Matthew Island Waters** (UGC: PKZ767). This includes coastal waters for Southwest Alaska, Bristol Bay, the Alaska Peninsula Waters, and the Aleutian Islands up to 100 nautical miles out.

### What You Should Do

Residents and mariners in the affected area are advised to **avoid** the region during the warning period. Heavy freezing spray can lead to significant ice accumulation on vessels, creating hazardous conditions and impacting stability.

### Expected Conditions

Weather conditions are expected to deteriorate starting Monday night with the following specifics provided by the NWS:

*   **Tuesday**: East winds of 40 knots east of Saint Matthew Island and Northeast winds of 50 knots west of the island. Seas in ice-free waters are expected to reach 20 feet. Widespread snow showers and heavy freezing spray are forecast.
*   **Tuesday Night**: East winds of 35 knots east of the island and Northeast winds of 45 knots west of the island. Seas in ice-free waters are expected to reach 21 feet with continued widespread snow showers and heavy freezing spray.
*   **Wednesday**: North winds of 45 knots with seas in ice-free waters at 19 feet.

### Timeline

*   **Onset**: The Heavy Freezing Spray Warning begins at 5:00 AM AKST on Tuesday, February 10, 2026.
*   **Duration**: The warning remains in effect until 5:00 AM AKST on Wednesday, February 11, 2026.
