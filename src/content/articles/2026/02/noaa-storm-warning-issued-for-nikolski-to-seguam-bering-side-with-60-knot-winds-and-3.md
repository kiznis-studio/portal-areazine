### Alert Details

A Storm Warning (SRW) has been issued by the National Weather Service in Anchorage, AK. The alert is currently in effect and is scheduled to remain active until 5:00 AM AKST on Wednesday, February 11.

### Affected Areas

The warning specifically covers the coastal waters from Nikolski to Seguam on the Bering Side, extending from 15 to 85 nautical miles out (UGC: PKZ776).

### What You Should Do

The National Weather Service advises a response of "Avoid" for the affected maritime areas. Mariners and residents in the region should take immediate precautions to protect life and property. Avoid travel in the warning area due to hazardous wind and sea conditions.

### Expected Conditions

Extreme maritime conditions are forecast for the duration of the alert:

*   **Today**: Southwest winds reaching 60 knots with seas as high as 35 feet. Snow showers and rain showers are expected.
*   **Tonight**: Southwest winds decreasing slightly to 50 knots with seas of 29 feet. Widespread snow and rain showers will persist.
*   **Wednesday**: West winds of 40 knots with seas of 22 feet and widespread snow showers.
*   **Extended Outlook**: Conditions are expected to remain hazardous through the week, with winds between 35 and 40 knots and seas ranging from 16 to 24 feet through Friday.

### Timeline

The Storm Warning was issued at 3:11 AM AKST on Tuesday, February 10, and is effective through 5:00 AM AKST on Wednesday, February 11. Conditions are expected to be most severe during the day Tuesday and into Tuesday night.
